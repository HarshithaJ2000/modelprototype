Step 1 — QR Tag Every Item: Each of the 432+ items across all 12 BorrowBox hubs is assigned a unique QR code label (e.g. BB-0001 through BB-0432). Staff scan it once to register the item into the database with its name, category, hub, and baseline condition. From this moment on, every movement of the item is tracked automatically.
Step 2 — Scan Out at Checkout: When a patron collects a reserved item, the hub staff member scans the QR code using the Scan Out screen. The system records the Patron ID, checkout timestamp, return due date, and the item's condition at the time of lending. The patron is linked to the item in the database. This creates the accountability record that makes damage attribution possible.
Step 3 — System Monitors Due Dates: The system checks every active loan against today's date on each login. If a due date has passed and the item has not been scanned back in, the system automatically flags it as overdue and adds it to the Overdue Alerts dashboard. An automated reminder is sent to the patron. If the item remains missing for 30+ days, it is escalated to Critical status and staff are notified.
Step 4 — Scan In and Log Condition: When a patron returns an item, staff scan the QR code on the Scan In screen and select the return condition. The system compares this against the checkout condition. If damage is detected, the item is automatically flagged Out of Service and the patron's record is updated. Items in Good condition return to Available inventory immediately — no manual update needed.
Step 5 — Data Builds Over Time: Every scan adds to the item's history. Over time, administrators can see which items degrade fastest, which patrons have repeat damage incidents, which hubs have the worst return rates, and which seasons see the most losses. This data drives evidence-based policy changes — such as requiring deposits for high-risk items or restricting certain categories to verified patrons.
